## **1. Introduction: Mobile Apps Without Mobile Developers**

Mobile app development is expensive. Hiring iOS and Android developers costs $100,000-300,000 per year, and building even a simple app takes 3-6 months. Adalo promises to democratize mobile development by letting non-technical users build native-feeling mobile apps through a visual drag-and-drop builder. After four months of testing, the promise is partially delivered.

Our service booking app—with user accounts, appointment scheduling, push notifications, and payment processing—was functional within three weeks. It works. Users can book services, receive confirmations, and pay through the app. But the performance doesn't match what a native developer would produce: screens take 1-3 seconds to load (native apps load instantly), animations aren't as smooth, and the overall experience feels like a web view rather than a truly native app.

Adalo was founded in 2018 by David Adkin and Ben Haefele. The platform generates mobile apps that can be published to the Apple App Store and Google Play Store—a genuine differentiator against web-app-focused tools like Bubble and Glide. The app wraps around a web view internally, which is why performance doesn't match native development, but for MVP validation and simple business apps, the tradeoff is acceptable.

The honest assessment: Adalo is the best no-code tool for building mobile app MVPs when you need app store distribution. It's not the best no-code tool for building the best mobile experience—that distinction belongs to FlutterFlow (which generates real Flutter code) or custom development.

## **2. What is Adalo?**

Adalo is a no-code mobile and web application builder that creates apps publishable to the iOS App Store and Google Play Store. The platform provides a drag-and-drop designer, a built-in database, user authentication, push notifications, and app store submission tools.

The platform uses a component-based approach where you place screens, connect them with navigation, add data components (lists, forms, detail views), and configure actions (click → navigate, submit → create record). The visual model is intuitive for simple apps but becomes complex for sophisticated interactions.

Adalo's database is built in—you define Collections (equivalent to tables) with Properties (fields) and establish relationships between Collections. The database handles basic CRUD operations, user authentication, and role-based access. For apps needing external data, Adalo connects to external APIs and Airtable.

## **4. Key Features**

### **4.1 Visual App Builder**

\[SCREENSHOT: Adalo's drag-and-drop editor with a booking screen being designed\]

Adalo's visual builder places components onto screens and connects them with navigation. You design each screen individually, add list components connected to database collections, configure forms for data submission, and set up navigation flows between screens. The building experience is intuitive for simple apps—our team directory app was built in under 4 hours with no prior Adalo experience.

The design quality of Adalo apps is decent but not premium. Components look clean but lack the visual sophistication of hand-designed interfaces. Custom styling options exist (colors, fonts, spacing) but the component library is more limited than Bubble's, and achieving unique designs requires workarounds.

### **4.2 App Store Publishing**

\[SCREENSHOT: App store submission interface showing iOS and Android configuration\]

Adalo handles the compilation and submission process for both iOS and Android app stores. You configure app name, icons, splash screens, and submission details within Adalo. The platform generates the binary files and handles the technical submission requirements. Our service booking app was approved on both stores within 5 days of submission.

The app store publishing is Adalo's primary differentiator against web-app-only tools. For businesses needing an app store presence, this is the simplest path without developers.

### **4.3 Push Notifications**

Push notifications (Professional plan) send alerts to users' devices—essential for engagement. Our booking app sends appointment reminders, booking confirmations, and promotional messages. The notifications work reliably on both iOS and Android.

### **4.4 Built-In Database**

Adalo's database handles standard CRUD operations with user authentication and basic role-based access. The database is simpler than Bubble's but sufficient for apps with straightforward data needs.

## **6. Adalo Cons**

### **Performance Is Noticeably Non-Native** - Screen loads of 1-3 seconds, occasionally janky animations, and an overall feel that experienced mobile users will recognize as "not native." For MVPs this is acceptable; for consumer apps competing with native apps, it's a disadvantage.

### **Limited Component Library** - Fewer components and less customization than Bubble. Complex UI patterns require workarounds or custom components (which require development knowledge).

### **Database Is Basic** - No complex queries, limited computed fields, and performance degrades with larger datasets. Apps with sophisticated data needs quickly outgrow Adalo's built-in database.

### **No Offline Support** - Apps require internet connectivity. Field apps, transit apps, and scenarios with inconsistent connectivity don't work well.

### **Platform Stability Concerns** - Adalo has experienced financial challenges (layoffs, reduced updates). The long-term platform viability should factor into decisions for apps intended to operate for years.

### **Pricing Requires Professional for Core Feature** - App store publishing—the primary reason to choose Adalo—requires $65/month minimum.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Is Adalo free?**
Free to build and test. Publishing to app stores requires Professional ($65/month).

### **Are Adalo apps truly native?**
No—they use web views wrapped in native containers. Performance is noticeably below native apps but adequate for MVPs and simple business apps.

### **Can Adalo publish to both iOS and Android?**
Yes, from a single project. Both app stores require the Professional plan.

### **How does Adalo compare to FlutterFlow?**
FlutterFlow produces better-quality apps (real Flutter code) with more customization. Adalo is simpler for beginners. Choose based on technical comfort and quality requirements.

### **Is Adalo still being actively developed?**
Yes, but the company has experienced financial challenges. Monitor the platform's update frequency before committing to long-term projects.
