## **1. Introduction: SAP's No-Code Play**

AppGyver was acquired by SAP in 2021 and rebranded as SAP Build Apps. The acquisition defines the platform: SAP Build Apps is free (remarkably generous for SAP) and integrates natively with SAP's Business Technology Platform, making it the natural no-code choice for organizations running SAP systems.

After three months building two SAP-connected applications, I found genuine capability for SAP-ecosystem apps. The visual builder is powerful, the SAP integration is native, and the price is unbeatable. But outside the SAP ecosystem, the platform competes poorly against Bubble, Glide, and other independent no-code tools due to a small community, enterprise-dense UX, and documentation that assumes SAP familiarity.

If you're an SAP shop, SAP Build Apps is a strong free option. If you're not, look elsewhere.

## **2. Platform & Features**

SAP Build Apps provides a component-based visual builder with logic flows, data integration with SAP and non-SAP sources (REST APIs), and deployment to web, iOS, and Android. The SAP integration—native connectivity to S/4HANA, SuccessFactors, and other SAP products—is the platform's strongest capability, enabling apps that read from and write to SAP systems without coding or middleware.

**Pricing:** Free for community use. Enterprise features require SAP BTP licensing (custom pricing, typically bundled with SAP contracts).

## **3. Pros & Cons**

Free pricing for an enterprise platform is remarkable. Native SAP integration eliminates middleware complexity. The visual builder handles genuine application logic with drag-and-drop flows. Cross-platform deployment covers web, iOS, and Android.

The downsides: small community compared to Bubble or Glide. Enterprise-dense UX that feels heavy compared to modern no-code tools. Steep learning curve for non-SAP users. Documentation assumes SAP ecosystem knowledge.

## **4. Verdict**

### **Overall Rating: 3.5/5**

SAP Build Apps is the right choice for SAP organizations wanting free no-code app development. Outside the SAP ecosystem, independent platforms provide better experiences. The free pricing is genuinely remarkable; the limited community and enterprise-dense UX are the tradeoffs.

**Best For:** SAP organizations building internal apps with native SAP integration.

**Not Recommended For:** Non-SAP organizations or teams wanting vibrant community support.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Is SAP Build Apps free?**
Yes for community/individual use. Enterprise features require SAP BTP licensing.

### **Is AppGyver the same as SAP Build Apps?**
Yes—SAP acquired AppGyver in 2021 and rebranded it.

### **Does SAP Build Apps require SAP?**
No—it works with REST APIs too. But the primary value is SAP integration.
