## **2. What is Beehiiv?**

Beehiiv is a newsletter platform designed for creators building audience-driven businesses. The platform combines newsletter writing and sending, website/blog publishing with SEO optimization, referral programs for viral growth, recommendation network (cross-promotion between newsletters), paid subscriptions and monetization, and audience analytics with growth metrics.

Unlike ConvertKit (which is an email marketing platform serving creators) or Mailchimp (which is a general email marketing tool), Beehiiv is a newsletter platform — the distinction matters more than it sounds. Beehiiv's strengths are in publishing (SEO-optimized web pages), growth (referral programs, recommendation network), and monetization (ad network, paid subscriptions, sponsorships). The question isn't "which is better?" but "what are you building?" If you're building a newsletter business where the newsletter IS the product, Beehiiv provides the growth and monetization infrastructure. If you're building a creator business that includes email as one of several channels (alongside courses, coaching, digital products), ConvertKit provides the broader email marketing capabilities.

## **4. Key Features**

### **4.1 Referral Program — The Viral Growth Engine**

\[SCREENSHOT: Referral program dashboard showing milestones and subscriber growth\]

The built-in referral program is Beehiiv's most powerful growth feature and the primary reason many creators choose the platform. Each subscriber receives a unique referral link. You configure milestone rewards — at 3 referrals, subscribers unlock exclusive content; at 5 referrals, they get a discount code; at 10 referrals, they receive a premium newsletter subscription; at 25 referrals, they get a free product or coaching call.

Our referral program configuration: 3 referrals = exclusive weekly deep-dive article; 5 referrals = access to our resource library; 10 referrals = premium paid newsletter subscription (valued at $7/month). This structure generated 23% of our total subscriber growth during the four-month evaluation — 1,840 subscribers who came through referral links rather than organic discovery.

The psychology works because subscribers become invested in sharing when they can see their progress toward the next reward. The referral dashboard shows each subscriber their unique link, current referral count, and distance to the next milestone. This gamification creates consistent sharing behavior that traditional "share this newsletter" buttons can't match.

### **4.2 Recommendation Network — Free Cross-Promotion**

\[SCREENSHOT: Recommendation setup showing cross-promotion with other newsletters\]

After a reader subscribes to your newsletter, Beehiiv displays a page recommending other newsletters they might enjoy — and your newsletter gets recommended to subscribers of complementary publications. This creates a cross-promotion network where growing newsletters help each other grow, without any cost or effort beyond initial configuration.

We configured recommendations with 5 complementary newsletters in our niche. Over four months, the recommendation network generated 400+ new subscribers — people who subscribed to a similar newsletter and then discovered ours through the recommendation page. This growth was entirely free, entirely passive, and entirely incremental to our other subscriber acquisition channels.

The recommendation network works because the timing is perfect — showing newsletter recommendations immediately after someone subscribes (when they're in "discovery mode") produces higher conversion rates than cold email or social media promotion. The quality of recommended subscribers tends to be high because they've already demonstrated interest in content similar to yours.

### **4.3 Web Publishing and SEO — Beyond the Inbox**

\[SCREENSHOT: Newsletter archive page showing SEO-optimized web publishing\]

Every newsletter you send on Beehiiv automatically publishes as a web page with proper SEO optimization — meta titles and descriptions, clean URLs, structured data markup, XML sitemap, and fast page load times. Unlike Substack (which hosts on substack.com subdomains), Beehiiv supports custom domains — your newsletter archive lives at newsletter.yourbrand.com, building your domain's SEO authority rather than Beehiiv's.

Our newsletter posts began ranking in Google search results within 6 weeks of consistent publishing. Topics we covered appeared in search results, driving organic traffic to our newsletter archive pages where visitors encountered subscribe CTAs alongside the content. This organic discovery channel generated approximately 200 subscribers per month from search traffic — subscribers who would never have found us through email-only distribution.

The web publishing transforms a newsletter from an email-only channel into a content platform with SEO-driven discovery. For creators who write about searchable topics (how-to content, industry analysis, product reviews), the web publishing creates a compounding growth channel that improves over time as your content library and domain authority grow.

### **4.4 Monetization — Multiple Revenue Streams**

\[SCREENSHOT: Monetization dashboard showing paid subscriptions, ad network, and revenue metrics\]

Beehiiv provides three monetization channels from a single platform:

**Paid subscriptions** through Stripe integration let you offer premium newsletter tiers. Free subscribers receive your regular newsletter; paid subscribers ($5-25/month, you set the price) receive premium content, additional editions, or exclusive analysis. Beehiiv takes no revenue share on Scale and Max plans — 100% of subscription revenue (minus Stripe's processing fee) goes to you. Our paid newsletter tier generated $600/month from 120 paid subscribers at $5/month.

**The Beehiiv Ad Network** matches advertisers with newsletters based on audience demographics and content category. Ads appear as native sponsorship blocks within your newsletter — clean, unobtrusive, and relevant to your audience. The ad network revenue is passive — Beehiiv handles advertiser matching, ad placement, and payment. Our ad network revenue averaged $350/month, covering the platform cost 7x over without any outreach to advertisers.

**Sponsorship marketplace** connects newsletter creators directly with brands looking for newsletter sponsorship. Unlike the ad network (Beehiiv manages everything), the marketplace facilitates direct relationships between creators and sponsors. This channel serves larger newsletters where custom sponsorship packages provide more revenue than programmatic ad placement.

### **4.5 Analytics — Growth-Focused Metrics**

\[SCREENSHOT: Analytics dashboard showing subscriber growth, referral performance, and engagement trends\]

Beehiiv's analytics focus on growth and engagement rather than marketing automation metrics. The dashboard provides:

**Subscriber growth** over time with source attribution — organic signups, referral program, recommendation network, import, and custom tracking links. Understanding where subscribers come from informs growth strategy investment.

**Engagement rates** by edition — open rate, click rate, and unique engagement per newsletter send. Trending engagement data shows whether your content is becoming more or less engaging over time.

**Referral program performance** — referrals by subscriber (who's sharing most), milestone progress (how close subscribers are to rewards), and viral coefficient (average referrals per subscriber).

**Revenue metrics** — paid subscriber count and revenue, ad network earnings, and total newsletter revenue. The revenue dashboard makes it easy to see your newsletter's business performance at a glance.

The subscriber source attribution proved particularly valuable for our growth strategy. Knowing that 23% of growth came from referrals, 15% from the recommendation network, 25% from organic search, and 37% from direct signups informed where we invested effort. We doubled down on creating referral-worthy content (content so good that readers wanted to share it) and optimizing SEO for our web-published archives based on this data.

## **6. Beehiiv Cons**

### **Not a Full Email Marketing Platform — By Design**

No multi-step automation workflows, no behavioral trigger sequences, no lead scoring, no CRM. Beehiiv sends newsletters and manages subscriber growth; it doesn't run marketing automation campaigns. Creators who need "when subscriber clicks link X, wait 2 days, then send email Y" workflows need ActiveCampaign or ConvertKit alongside or instead of Beehiiv. The basic welcome sequence automation exists, but sophisticated email marketing requires a different platform.

### **Limited Segmentation Compared to Marketing Platforms**

Basic segments work well — engagement-based (active vs. inactive subscribers), subscription type (free vs. paid), and referral activity. But advanced behavioral segmentation (sent email sequence → clicked specific link → visited specific page → tag with interest → trigger targeted campaign) doesn't match ActiveCampaign, Klaviyo, or even ConvertKit. For newsletter creators sending the same content to their entire list, this limitation doesn't matter. For creators wanting targeted content to different audience segments, the segmentation ceiling is real.

### **Young Platform Still Maturing**

Founded 2021 — less battle-tested than ConvertKit (2013), Mailchimp (2001), or even Substack (2017). Features are still being built, and the platform occasionally shows its youth through missing features, UI rough edges, or documentation gaps. The development velocity is fast (new features ship frequently), but early adopters accept the tradeoff of innovation speed vs. platform maturity.

### **Creator Commerce Is Narrower Than ConvertKit**

Paid subscriptions and ad network revenue only. No digital product sales (ebooks, courses, templates), no tip jars, no coaching package sales. ConvertKit's commerce is significantly broader — if you sell digital products alongside your newsletter, ConvertKit handles both while Beehiiv only handles the newsletter monetization.

### **Design Flexibility Is Intentionally Limited**

The newsletter format emphasizes clean, readable content over elaborate design. Creators wanting highly designed email campaigns with custom layouts, image-heavy designs, and branded templates have fewer options than Mailchimp provides. Beehiiv's philosophy is that great newsletters succeed through writing quality, not design complexity — which is true for most newsletters but limiting for creators who want visual distinctiveness.

### **Can I monetize my newsletter on Beehiiv?**
Yes — three monetization channels: paid subscriptions (Stripe integration, no revenue share on Scale+), the Beehiiv Ad Network (advertisers matched to your audience), and direct sponsorship marketplace. Multiple revenue streams from a single platform.

### **Is Beehiiv better than ConvertKit for newsletters?**
For newsletters specifically: yes. Beehiiv has better growth tools (referral programs, recommendation network), better web publishing (SEO-optimized archives), and cheaper pricing at scale (unlimited subscribers at $49/month). For broader creator email marketing (automation, digital products, sequences): ConvertKit is more capable.

### **What is the Beehiiv recommendation network?**
After a new subscriber joins your newsletter, Beehiiv recommends other newsletters they might enjoy — and your newsletter gets recommended to subscribers of complementary publications. This creates free cross-promotion growth. We gained 400+ subscribers through recommendations over 4 months without any effort.

### **How does the Beehiiv referral program work?**
Each subscriber gets a unique referral link. You set milestone rewards — e.g., refer 3 friends to get exclusive content, refer 10 to get premium access. Subscribers share their link, and when friends subscribe, the referrer's count increases toward the next milestone. The gamification drives viral growth.

### **Can I use a custom domain with Beehiiv?**
Yes, on Scale ($49/month) and above. Your newsletter publishes at your own domain (newsletter.yourbrand.com) rather than a Beehiiv subdomain. This gives you full brand control and SEO benefits from content published on your own domain.

### **Does Beehiiv have A/B testing?**
Yes — subject line A/B testing on Scale plans, with content A/B testing on Max plans. Test subject lines with a portion of your audience and send the winner to the rest. This optimization capability improves open rates over time.
