## **1. Introduction**

Betty Blocks is a European no-code platform founded in 2012 in the Netherlands, targeting enterprise citizen development programs. The platform enables business users to build applications with IT governance—non-developers create solutions while IT maintains control over security, data, and deployment.

After two months evaluating Betty Blocks alongside OutSystems and Mendix, I found a platform serving a specific niche: European enterprises wanting GDPR-compliant no-code with strong citizen development governance. The platform lacks the depth of OutSystems or the community of Bubble, but for organizations prioritizing European data residency and business-user accessibility with IT oversight, Betty Blocks fills a genuine gap.

The platform provides a visual builder with drag-and-drop UI, data modeling, business logic flows, and API integrations. The "citizen development" positioning means the building experience is designed for non-technical users—simpler than OutSystems or Mendix but more governed than Bubble or Glide.

Betty Blocks differentiates through European identity (EU data centers, GDPR focus), citizen development governance, and multi-experience support. The enterprise pricing and European focus create a narrow but defensible market position.

## **2. Verdict**

### **Overall Rating: 3.4/5**

Betty Blocks serves European enterprises wanting governed citizen development. Outside that niche, more capable and accessible alternatives exist. The European identity and governance model are genuine differentiators for the right organizations.

**Best For:** European enterprises, citizen development programs.

**Not Recommended For:** SMBs, non-European organizations, or teams wanting the most capable no-code builder.
