## **2. What is Brevo? Understanding the Platform**

\[VISUAL: Brevo platform overview showing email, SMS, WhatsApp, CRM, and automation\]

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform combining email marketing, SMS, WhatsApp Business messaging, marketing automation, CRM, landing pages, transactional email, live chat, and meeting scheduling into a single platform. The breadth of channels at this pricing is unique in the market — no other platform offers email + SMS + WhatsApp + CRM + transactional email at Brevo's price point.

The platform differentiates from Mailchimp (more channels, dramatically better pricing at scale, but fewer templates), ActiveCampaign (cheaper, multi-channel, but less sophisticated automation), and Klaviyo (dramatically cheaper, not e-commerce-specific, but multi-channel). The "all-in-one at affordable pricing" positioning serves SMBs that want marketing capabilities across channels without assembling separate tools or paying enterprise prices.

The email-based pricing model (pay per send, not per contact) is the strategic differentiator worth understanding deeply. Traditional email platforms charge based on your total contact count — every contact in your database costs money whether you email them weekly or haven't contacted them in a year. Brevo charges based on emails actually sent. This model particularly benefits businesses with large databases but moderate sending frequency — a newsletter to 50,000 people once a week is dramatically cheaper on Brevo than on contact-based platforms, and maintaining inactive contacts for future campaigns doesn't inflate your bill.

Brevo's product suite has expanded significantly since its Sendinblue days. The 2023 rebrand reflected a strategic shift from "email marketing tool" to "marketing platform" — adding CRM, WhatsApp, meetings, and enhanced automation alongside the core email product. The breadth is impressive for the price, though depth in any single area doesn't match best-in-class competitors.

## **3. Brevo Pricing & Plans: The Pay-Per-Email Revolution**

\[VISUAL: Pricing comparison showing per-contact vs per-email models\]

Understanding Brevo's pricing requires understanding how radically different its model is from the industry standard. Most email platforms charge $X per contact per month. Brevo charges $X per email sent per month, with unlimited contacts on every plan including free.

### **3.1 Free Plan (300 Emails/Day, Unlimited Contacts) — The Best Free Plan in Email**

The free plan sends up to 300 emails per day (approximately 9,000/month) to unlimited contacts. It includes marketing automation for up to 2,000 contacts in workflows, landing pages, signup forms, and basic reporting. Brevo branding appears on sent emails. This plan is genuinely useful — not a trial, not a feature-limited teaser, but a permanent free tier that small businesses can operate on indefinitely.

We tested the free plan for the first month to evaluate the platform before committing budget. The 300/day limit constrained our sending but didn't prevent us from building campaigns, testing automation workflows, and evaluating deliverability. For businesses with under 5,000 contacts sending 2-3 campaigns per month, the free plan handles the workload.

### **3.2 Starter ($25/month for 20,000 Emails) — Growing Businesses**

Starter removes the daily sending limit and Brevo branding, adds basic reporting, and provides email support. At $25/month for 20,000 emails to unlimited contacts, the value is extraordinary. A business with 10,000 contacts sending 2 campaigns per month uses 20,000 emails — costing $25/month on Brevo vs. $100+/month on Mailchimp.

### **3.3 Business ($65/month for 20,000 Emails) — Full Marketing Platform**

Business adds marketing automation (unlimited contacts in workflows), A/B testing, advanced statistics, multi-user access, send time optimization, phone support, and landing pages. This is where most growing businesses should operate. The $65/month price for 20,000 emails with full automation access competes favorably against platforms charging $100-300/month for similar capabilities.

Additional email volume is available in blocks: 40,000 emails for $45/month additional, 60,000 for $69/month, scaling up from there. Our 800K emails over 5 months averaged roughly $130/month on the Business plan with volume add-ons — dramatically less than comparable usage on contact-based platforms.

### **3.4 Enterprise (Custom Pricing) — Scale and Support**

Dedicated IP, advanced integrations, SSO, dedicated account manager, sub-account management, priority support, and SLA. For organizations with complex requirements or very high volume.

## **4. Key Features Deep Dive**

### **4.1 Email Marketing — Solid Core with Room to Grow**

\[SCREENSHOT: Drag-and-drop email editor with template gallery\]

The email builder uses a drag-and-drop editor with pre-built templates. The template library offers 60+ designs covering newsletters, promotions, announcements, and transactional layouts. While smaller than Mailchimp's 100+ collection, the templates are modern, mobile-responsive, and customizable. The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive — our newest marketing team member was building campaigns independently within her first day.

Our campaigns achieved 91% inbox placement across major email providers — slightly below Mailchimp (93%) and ActiveCampaign (94%) but within acceptable range for effective email marketing. The deliverability tools include dedicated IP address (Business plan and above), authentication setup guides (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), and engagement-based sending reputation management.

A/B testing supports subject lines, content variations, sender name, and send time optimization. The send time optimization feature — which analyzes when each contact is most likely to open and delivers accordingly — improved our open rates by 12% compared to fixed-time sending. This feature alone justified the Business plan upgrade.

### **4.2 SMS and WhatsApp Marketing — True Multi-Channel**

\[SCREENSHOT: SMS campaign builder alongside email campaign\]

Brevo's multi-channel capability is its second strongest differentiator after pricing. SMS campaigns send directly from the same platform as email, using the same contact database and segmentation. No separate SMS tool, no integration to maintain, no data sync to manage. Create a segment, send an email to the segment, and send an SMS to the same segment — all from one interface.

Our SMS campaigns achieved 98% delivery rates and 22% click-through rates — dramatically higher than email's 3-4% CTR. For time-sensitive promotions (flash sales, event reminders, appointment confirmations), SMS consistently outperformed email. The SMS cost ($0.01-0.015 per message in the US, higher internationally) adds to the base subscription, but the integrated campaign management and shared analytics make it more efficient than separate SMS tools.

WhatsApp Business messaging enables conversational marketing on the world's most-used messaging platform. We tested WhatsApp campaigns for promotional messages and saw 45% open rates — significantly higher than both email and SMS. The WhatsApp integration is newer and less mature than the email and SMS features, but for businesses with audiences in WhatsApp-dominant markets (Latin America, Europe, Asia), the capability is genuinely differentiated.

### **4.3 Marketing Automation — Functional, Not Fancy**

\[SCREENSHOT: Automation workflow builder showing a welcome series with conditional splits\]

The automation builder handles standard marketing workflows: welcome series, abandoned cart recovery (with Shopify, WooCommerce integrations), date-based campaigns (birthdays, anniversaries), behavioral triggers (page visits, link clicks), and lead scoring. The visual builder is clear and functional — you can build multi-step workflows with delays, conditions, and branching without confusion.

We built 15 automations covering welcome sequences (5-email series converting 8% of new subscribers), lead nurturing (educational content series for marketing qualified leads), birthday campaigns (personalized discount with 15% redemption rate), and re-engagement (targeting contacts inactive for 90+ days). The automations performed reliably and generated measurable results across all five months.

The limitation compared to ActiveCampaign shows in conditional complexity. ActiveCampaign supports nested if/else conditions with multiple criteria, goal-based exits, and sophisticated branching logic. Brevo's conditions are more straightforward — single-level if/else with basic criteria. For most SMB automation needs (welcome, nurture, re-engage, recover cart), Brevo's automation is sufficient. For complex marketing operations with sophisticated lead scoring, multi-path journeys, and goal-based optimization, ActiveCampaign's depth matters.

### **4.4 Transactional Email — Genuinely Best-in-Class**

\[SCREENSHOT: Transactional email dashboard showing delivery rates and API documentation\]

Brevo's transactional email service (order confirmations, password resets, shipping notifications, account verifications) is genuinely excellent — one of the best in the market and a significant reason businesses choose the platform. The SMTP relay and API are well-documented with client libraries for every major language (Node.js, Python, PHP, Ruby, Go, Java). Deliverability for transactional emails averages 98%+ inbox placement, which is critical for messages that users actively expect and need.

Many businesses use Brevo specifically for transactional email alongside another platform for marketing — the transactional service stands alone as a competitor to dedicated platforms like SendGrid, Postmark, and Mailgun. The pricing includes transactional sends in your email volume (or a separate transactional-only plan starting at $15/month for 20,000 emails), making it cost-effective to consolidate marketing and transactional email on one platform.

### **4.5 CRM — Basic but Functional**

A basic CRM with deal pipeline, contact management, task tracking, and activity logging. The CRM is comparable in scope to ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM — adequate for small sales teams managing a simple pipeline but not a replacement for HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Salesforce. We used the CRM for tracking leads generated through our email campaigns, and it handled the basic workflow (lead → qualified → proposal → closed) without issues. The CRM's integration with email marketing is where it adds value — you can see a contact's full email engagement history (opens, clicks, conversions) alongside their deal stage, which provides context for sales conversations. Don't expect advanced features like forecasting, territory management, or custom objects — for those needs, pair Brevo with a dedicated CRM like HubSpot or Pipedrive.

## **6. Brevo Cons: Where It Falls Short**

### **Automation Trails ActiveCampaign**

The automation builder handles standard workflows well but lacks ActiveCampaign's conditional depth, goal-based exits, and visual workflow sophistication. Teams with complex marketing operations that require multi-path customer journeys with nested conditions will find the ceiling frustrating. The gap isn't in basic automation (Brevo handles that fine) but in advanced automation logic.

### **Template Library Is Smaller and Less Polished**

60+ templates vs. Mailchimp's 100+, and the design quality doesn't match Mailchimp's polish. Brevo's templates are functional and modern, but teams with strong brand design standards may find themselves building custom templates rather than customizing existing ones. The email editor itself is solid — the limitation is in the starting template variety.

### **Deliverability Is Good, Not Best-in-Class**

Our 91% inbox placement is acceptable for effective email marketing, but it trails Mailchimp (93%) and ActiveCampaign (94%). The 2-3% gap matters at scale — on 100,000 emails, that's 2,000-3,000 fewer inboxed messages. Dedicated IP (Business plan and above) helps improve this, but the out-of-box deliverability doesn't match the market leaders.

### **Reporting Is Basic Compared to Leaders**

Campaign metrics (opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes) and basic automation reporting cover standard needs. But advanced analytics, attribution modeling, custom report building, and cross-campaign analysis don't match what HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, or even Mailchimp offer. Data-driven marketing teams that optimize based on detailed analytics will find Brevo's reporting insufficient.

### **SMS and WhatsApp Costs Add Up**

While SMS and WhatsApp are available in the platform, they're billed per message on top of the email plan. SMS at $0.01-0.015/message and WhatsApp at $0.02-0.05/message create additional costs that can surprise teams planning aggressive multi-channel campaigns. A campaign to 10,000 contacts via SMS adds $100-150 to your monthly bill.

### **How does Brevo's pricing work?**
Brevo charges per email sent, not per contact stored. Unlimited contacts on every plan including free. You choose a monthly email volume tier ($25/month for 20,000 emails, $65/month for 20,000 emails with automation, scaling up from there). This is fundamentally different from Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign, which charge based on your total contact count.

### **How does Brevo compare to Mailchimp?**
Brevo is dramatically cheaper at scale (pay per email vs per contact — saving 50-80% with large contact lists). Mailchimp has better templates (100+ vs 60+), slightly higher deliverability (93% vs 91%), and a more polished overall experience. Choose based on whether budget or design polish matters more to your team.

### **Does Brevo support SMS and WhatsApp marketing?**
Yes — SMS and WhatsApp Business messaging from the same platform, using the same contact database and segmentation. Messages are billed per send (SMS: $0.01-0.015, WhatsApp: $0.02-0.05) on top of the email plan. Campaign creation, audience targeting, and analytics are unified across all channels.

### **Is Brevo good for transactional email?**
Excellent — one of the best transactional email services available, rivaling dedicated platforms like SendGrid and Postmark. Well-documented API with client libraries for all major programming languages. 98%+ inbox placement for transactional sends. Can be used alongside the marketing platform or as a standalone transactional service.

### **How does Brevo's automation compare to ActiveCampaign?**
Brevo handles standard automation workflows well (welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement, birthday campaigns). ActiveCampaign offers more sophisticated conditional logic, goal-based exits, nested conditions, and visual workflow complexity. For most SMB automation needs, Brevo is sufficient. For advanced marketing operations, ActiveCampaign's depth matters.

### **Is Brevo GDPR compliant?**
Yes — Brevo is headquartered in France with GDPR compliance built into the platform's architecture. Double opt-in enforcement, consent management, data portability, right-to-erasure, and data processing agreements are standard features. European data centers are available for data residency requirements.

### **Can Brevo handle e-commerce email marketing?**
Brevo integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, and PrestaShop for basic e-commerce automation (abandoned cart, purchase confirmation, customer segmentation). However, the e-commerce data integration isn't as deep as Klaviyo's — no predictive analytics, no product recommendations, no purchase-behavior segmentation. For serious e-commerce email marketing, Klaviyo is the better choice.

### **What's the deliverability like on Brevo?**
Our testing showed 91% inbox placement — good but slightly below Mailchimp (93%) and ActiveCampaign (94%). Dedicated IP (available on Business plan) improves deliverability. Authentication setup (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) is straightforward with clear guides. For most businesses, 91% inbox placement is adequate for effective email marketing.

### **Does Brevo have a CRM?**
Yes — a basic CRM with deal pipeline, contact management, task tracking, and activity logging. Adequate for small sales teams managing a simple pipeline. Not a replacement for HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Salesforce. The CRM integrates with email and automation, allowing trigger-based workflows when deals move through stages.

### **Can I migrate from Mailchimp to Brevo?**
Yes. Brevo provides migration guides for Mailchimp, including contact export/import with field mapping. Email templates need to be rebuilt in Brevo's editor (they don't transfer directly). Automation workflows need to be recreated. Plan for 1-2 weeks of migration effort, with the primary work being template and automation recreation rather than data transfer.

### **Does Brevo have a mobile app?**
Yes, for both iOS and Android. The mobile app provides campaign performance monitoring, contact management, and basic campaign creation. It's useful for checking campaign results on the go but not a replacement for the desktop experience for building campaigns or configuring automation workflows.

### **How does Brevo handle list management and segmentation?**
Brevo supports list-based and attribute-based segmentation. Create static lists (manually curated) or dynamic segments (automatically updated based on contact attributes, engagement behavior, or custom conditions). Segments can combine multiple criteria — purchase history, email engagement, geographic location, custom fields — to target specific audiences. The segmentation is solid for SMB needs, though not as deep as Klaviyo's e-commerce-specific behavioral segmentation.
