## **1. Introduction: Unlimited Contacts, Pay Per Email**

Brevo's pricing model is its most disruptive feature: you pay based on how many emails you send, not how many contacts you have. This fundamental difference means a business with 50,000 contacts on Brevo's Business plan pays $65/month—while the same business on Mailchimp pays $410/month and on ActiveCampaign pays $299/month. The savings are extraordinary.

After five months running Brevo with a 12-person team managing 30,000+ contacts and sending 800K+ emails, I found a platform that delivers strong core email marketing with genuine multi-channel capabilities (SMS, WhatsApp) at pricing that makes competitors look overpriced. The automation builder handles standard workflows well. The transactional email service is excellent. And the free plan—300 emails per day with unlimited contacts—is the most generous permanent free offering in email marketing.

Brevo was founded as Sendinblue in 2012 by Armand Thiberge in Paris, rebranding to Brevo in 2023. The company has raised over $160 million and serves 500,000+ businesses globally. The European headquarters means GDPR compliance is foundational, not an afterthought—important for businesses operating in or marketing to European markets.

The honest assessment: Brevo provides 75% of what ActiveCampaign offers at 30% of the cost, plus SMS and WhatsApp that ActiveCampaign charges separately for. The 25% gap shows in automation sophistication, template quality, and reporting depth. For businesses where budget is the primary constraint, that tradeoff is clearly worth making.

## **2. What is Brevo?**

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform combining email marketing, SMS, WhatsApp, marketing automation, CRM, landing pages, transactional email, and chat into a single platform. The breadth of channels at the pricing makes it unique in the market.

The platform differentiates from Mailchimp (more channels, better pricing at scale), ActiveCampaign (cheaper, multi-channel, less sophisticated automation), and Klaviyo (cheaper, not e-commerce-specific, multi-channel). The "all-in-one at affordable pricing" positioning serves SMBs that want marketing capabilities across channels without assembling separate tools.

The email-based pricing model (pay per send, not per contact) is the strategic differentiator. This model particularly benefits businesses with large databases but moderate sending frequency—a newsletter to 50,000 people once a week is dramatically cheaper on Brevo than on contact-based platforms.

## **4. Key Features**

### **4.1 Email Marketing**

The email builder uses drag-and-drop design with pre-built templates. The template library is smaller than Mailchimp's (60+ vs 100+) but covers common use cases. The editor is intuitive, mobile preview works well, and A/B testing supports subject lines, content, and send times.

Our campaigns achieved 91% inbox placement—slightly below Mailchimp (93%) and ActiveCampaign (94%) but within acceptable range. The deliverability tools include dedicated IP (Business+), authentication setup, and engagement-based sending reputation.

### **4.2 SMS & WhatsApp Marketing**

Brevo's multi-channel capability is its second strongest differentiator after pricing. SMS campaigns send directly from the same platform as email, using the same contact database and segmentation. WhatsApp Business messaging enables conversational marketing on the world's most-used messaging platform.

Our SMS campaigns achieved 98% delivery rates and 22% click-through rates—dramatically higher than email (3-4% CTR). The WhatsApp campaigns, while newer, generated strong engagement for promotional messages (45% open rate). Having all channels in one platform with shared contact data eliminates the integration headaches of separate tools.

### **4.3 Marketing Automation**

The automation builder handles standard workflows: welcome series, abandoned cart (with e-commerce integrations), date-based campaigns, and behavioral triggers. The visual builder is clear and functional—not as powerful as ActiveCampaign's conditional logic but adequate for most SMB automation needs.

We built 15 automations covering welcome sequences, lead nurturing, birthday campaigns, and re-engagement. The automations performed reliably and generated measurable results. The limitation compared to ActiveCampaign is conditional complexity—sophisticated if/else branching with multiple conditions requires workarounds.

### **4.4 Transactional Email**

Brevo's transactional email service (order confirmations, password resets, shipping notifications) is genuinely excellent—one of the best in the market. The API is well-documented, deliverability is strong (98%+ inbox placement for transactional), and the pricing includes transactional sends in your email volume. Many businesses use Brevo specifically for transactional email alongside another platform for marketing.

### **4.5 CRM**

A basic CRM with deal pipeline, contact management, and task tracking. Similar in scope to ActiveCampaign's CRM—adequate for small sales teams but not a Salesforce or HubSpot replacement.

## **6. Brevo Cons**

### **Automation Trails ActiveCampaign** - The automation builder handles basics well but lacks the conditional depth and visual sophistication of ActiveCampaign's workflow engine.

### **Template Library Is Smaller** - 60+ templates vs Mailchimp's 100+. The designs are adequate but the variety doesn't match the market leader.

### **Deliverability Is Good, Not Best-in-Class** - 91% inbox placement is acceptable but below Mailchimp (93%) and ActiveCampaign (94%). Dedicated IP (Business+) helps.

### **Reporting Is Basic** - Campaign metrics and basic automation reporting. Advanced analytics, attribution modeling, and custom reporting don't match HubSpot or ActiveCampaign.

### **SMS/WhatsApp Costs Are Additional** - While available in the platform, SMS and WhatsApp messages are billed per message on top of the email plan. High-volume multi-channel campaigns create costs beyond the base subscription.
