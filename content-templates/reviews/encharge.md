## **1. Introduction**

Encharge is marketing automation built specifically for SaaS companies. While ActiveCampaign serves all business types and Customer.io requires engineering for event integration, Encharge targets the middle ground: SaaS-specific automation that marketing teams can configure without heavy engineering involvement.

After four months with a SaaS product managing 8,000 user profiles, I found Encharge handles the SaaS-specific workflows that general marketing tools struggle with: trial-to-paid conversion sequences triggered by product usage milestones, onboarding emails based on feature adoption, churn prevention triggered by declining engagement, and expansion prompts when users approach plan limits.

Encharge was founded in 2019 by Kaloyan Yankulov. The platform integrates with popular SaaS tools (Segment, Stripe, Intercom, HubSpot) to ingest product usage data and trigger marketing messages based on user behavior within your product.

The platform's SaaS-specific focus creates genuine value for its target audience. The trial conversion flow—which triggers different emails based on whether users completed onboarding steps, used key features, or showed buying signals—improved our trial-to-paid rate from 6% to 9.2%. That 3.2 percentage point improvement on 500 monthly trials represented $16,000/month in additional revenue.

## **2. Key Features & Verdict**

**SaaS-Specific Flow Builder:** Visual automation builder with triggers tied to product events (user signed up, feature used, plan limit reached, subscription changed). The triggers are pre-built for common SaaS scenarios—no custom API integration needed for basic usage tracking.

**User Segmentation:** Segments based on product usage, plan type, feature adoption, billing status, and engagement. "Free trial users who completed onboarding but haven't used Feature X in 7 days" creates precise targeting for SaaS-specific messaging.

**Integration Hub:** Native connections to Segment (product analytics), Stripe (billing events), Intercom (messaging), HubSpot (CRM), and Salesforce. The integrations bring product data into the marketing automation without custom engineering.

**Lead Scoring:** Score users based on product engagement and email interaction. Users who actively use the product score higher than users who signed up but never logged in—creating a behavior-based qualification model.

**Email Builder:** Standard drag-and-drop builder with templates. The email capabilities are comparable to ActiveCampaign—solid without being exceptional.

### **Overall Rating: 3.7/5**

Encharge fills a genuine gap for SaaS companies: marketing automation that understands product usage without requiring the engineering investment that Customer.io demands. The SaaS-specific flows, integrations, and segmentation serve startup and growth-stage SaaS companies well. The narrow focus and relatively small company size (compared to ActiveCampaign or HubSpot) create risk for organizations wanting the safest long-term platform choice.

**Best For:** SaaS startups (1,000-50,000 users) wanting product-behavior-driven marketing automation without heavy engineering.

**Not Recommended For:** Non-SaaS companies, enterprise organizations, or teams wanting the most established platform.
