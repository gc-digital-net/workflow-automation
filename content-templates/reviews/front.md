## **1. Introduction**

Front transforms shared email management from chaos to collaboration. If your team manages support@, sales@, info@, or any shared inbox, you know the problems: duplicate responses, missed emails, no visibility into who's handling what, and conversations falling through cracks. After four months managing 4 shared inboxes with a 10-person team, Front solved every one of those problems while feeling like a natural extension of email rather than a separate system.

Founded in 2013 by Mathilde Collin and Laurent Perrin in San Francisco, Front has raised over $200 million and serves over 8,500 businesses including Shopify, HubSpot, Airbnb, and Lyft. The platform sits between email clients (Gmail, Outlook) and help desks (Zendesk, Freshdesk)—providing the collaboration features of a help desk within the familiar email interface.

Front's approach is intentionally different from ticket-based systems. Conversations look and feel like email—no ticket numbers, no automated acknowledgments, no "your case has been escalated." The customer experience is a personal email conversation. Behind the scenes, the team has assignment rules, internal comments, SLA tracking, and analytics.

## **2. Key Features & Verdict**

**Shared Inboxes:** Multiple team members manage the same inbox without stepping on each other. Assignment, collision detection, and conversation history prevent duplicates and ensure accountability.

**Internal Comments:** Team discussion happens within the email thread—invisible to the customer. Our team used internal comments for quick questions ("should we offer a discount?") and approvals ("this response looks good, send it") without forwarding or separate chat.

**Rules & Automation:** Auto-assignment, auto-tagging, SLA alerts, and template responses. Our 15 rules handled routing (billing@ to finance team, support@ to support team) and escalation (no response after 4 hours → alert manager).

**Analytics:** Response time, resolution time, conversation volume, and team performance metrics. The analytics are more focused than Zendesk Explore but sufficient for managing team performance.

**Integrations:** CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), project management (Asana, Jira), and communication (Slack, Zoom) integrations bring context into conversations. The Salesforce integration pulls contact data into the conversation sidebar.

**Multi-Channel:** Email, SMS, chat (via integrations), social media, and WhatsApp. The multi-channel is growing but email remains the primary focus.

### **Overall Rating: 4.0/5**

Front is the best collaborative inbox for teams that manage shared email addresses. The email-like experience creates better customer interactions than ticket systems, while the collaboration features (assignment, comments, rules) create team coordination that plain email can't provide. For email-heavy support and operations teams, Front fills a genuine gap between email clients and help desks.

**Best For:** Teams managing shared inboxes (support, sales, operations), organizations where customer communication should feel personal rather than automated, and teams wanting help desk functionality in an email-native interface.

**Not Recommended For:** Teams wanting traditional ticketing (use Zendesk), AI chatbot-first support (use Intercom), or very small teams where a shared Gmail label suffices.
