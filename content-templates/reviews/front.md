## **1. Introduction: The Space Between Email and Help Desk**

There's a gap in the communication tool market that most people don't recognize until they fall into it. Email clients (Gmail, Outlook) handle individual communication beautifully but collapse when multiple people need to manage a shared inbox—duplicate responses, missed emails, no visibility into who's handling what. Help desks (Zendesk, Freshdesk) solve the shared inbox problem but replace the natural email experience with ticket numbers, system notifications, and automated workflows that make communication feel institutional rather than personal.

Front sits precisely in that gap. After four months managing 4 shared inboxes with a 10-person team—handling 6,000+ conversations monthly across customer support, sales inquiries, billing questions, and partnership communications—I found Front provides the collaborative coordination of a help desk within the familiar experience of email. No ticket numbers. No system-generated reply formatting. No "your request #48291 has been updated." Just email that happens to be managed by a team rather than an individual.

The distinction matters more than it sounds. When a customer emails your support@ address and receives a reply from "Sarah" that looks like a normal email—but behind the scenes, Sarah's response was drafted with input from an internal comment thread, approved by a manager through an @mention, and auto-logged to the customer's Salesforce record—that's Front working as designed. The customer experiences personal communication. The team experiences coordinated operations. Both get what they need.

Front was founded in 2013 by Mathilde Collin and Laurent Perrin in San Francisco. The company has raised over $200 million and serves over 8,500 businesses including Shopify, HubSpot, Airbnb, and Lyft. Front is positioned not as a help desk replacement but as a "customer operations platform"—serving the communication needs that sit between pure email and structured ticketing.

My testing framework evaluates collaborative communication tools across inbox management quality, team coordination features, customer experience, integration depth, reporting usefulness, and pricing value. Front scored at the top for team coordination and customer experience (the personal email feel), competitive on integrations and reporting, and lower on AI capabilities and multi-channel breadth (where Zendesk and Intercom offer more).

## **2. What is Front? Understanding the Platform**

\[VISUAL: Front's architecture showing shared inboxes, internal comments, rules, and integrations\]

Front is a collaborative email management platform that transforms how teams handle shared inboxes. The platform combines shared email management (multiple people working in the same inbox with coordination features), internal collaboration (comments and @mentions within email threads, invisible to the customer), automated rules (routing, tagging, and assignment based on configurable conditions), analytics (response time, team performance, conversation volume), and integrations (CRM, project management, communication tools).

What fundamentally separates Front from email clients is team coordination. Gmail's shared inbox approach (shared label or delegate access) creates chaos—multiple people see the same emails, there's no assignment mechanism, collision detection doesn't exist, and internal discussion requires forwarding or separate chat. Front adds assignment (one person owns each conversation), collision detection (see who else is viewing or drafting), internal comments (team discussion within the email thread, invisible to the customer), and shared drafts (collaboratively compose responses before sending).

What fundamentally separates Front from help desks is the customer experience. Zendesk and Freshdesk process emails through their systems, adding ticket numbers, automated footers, and system formatting that customers immediately recognize as "support ticket." Front sends emails that look exactly like personal email from the agent—same formatting, same signature, no system artifacts. The customer doesn't know (and doesn't need to know) that their email is managed through a team platform.

The platform serves teams across functions, not just customer support. Sales teams manage sales@ inboxes, coordinating prospect responses with internal strategy discussions. Operations teams manage vendor and partner communications. Finance teams handle invoice and payment inquiries. Legal teams coordinate contract responses. Any function that manages a shared email address benefits from Front's collaborative model.

Front has expanded beyond pure email into SMS, live chat (via integrations), social media, and WhatsApp—though email remains the primary channel and the core use case. The multi-channel capabilities are growing but don't yet match Zendesk's breadth.

## **4. Key Features Deep Dive**

### **4.1 Shared Inbox - Team Coordination Without the Chaos**

\[SCREENSHOT: Shared inbox showing conversations with assignment indicators, tags, and unread status\]

Front's shared inbox is the feature that justifies the platform's existence. Multiple team members access the same inbox (support@, sales@, billing@) with coordination features that prevent the collision, duplication, and confusion that plague shared Gmail accounts.

Every conversation has one owner—the assigned team member. Other team members see the assignment and know not to respond. If an unassigned conversation arrives, the team sees it in the shared queue and someone claims it—or an auto-assignment rule distributes it automatically. This simple ownership model eliminated the double-response problem that cost us 2-3 embarrassing duplicate replies per week on our previous shared Gmail setup.

The collision detection shows real-time activity: if another team member is viewing or drafting a response to the same conversation, Front displays their avatar and a "typing" indicator. During our evaluation, collision detection prevented approximately 15-20 potential duplicate responses per week—each one a customer experience save.

The inbox views organize conversations by status (open, snoozed, assigned, unassigned, closed), by tag (product areas, issue types, priority levels), or by custom filters. Our support inbox used views for "Unassigned" (conversations needing someone to claim them), "Waiting on Customer" (snoozed until customer responds), "Escalated" (tagged for senior agent attention), and "My Conversations" (each agent's assigned work). The views create order from the potential chaos of thousands of monthly conversations.

Shared drafts enable collaborative response composition before sending. An agent drafts a response, @mentions a colleague for review ("does this refund policy apply here?"), the colleague edits the draft or adds an internal comment confirming the approach, and the agent sends the finalized response. The customer receives one polished, accurate reply. Behind the scenes, three team members contributed. This collaborative drafting is impossible with standard email and the primary reason our team chose Front over Gmail with shared labels.

**What's Missing:** Front's inbox model is email-first. While chat and SMS channels are available, the experience is optimized for email conversations. Teams whose primary channel is live chat should evaluate Intercom or Crisp—Front's chat capabilities are functional but secondary.

### **4.2 Internal Comments - Team Collaboration Invisible to Customers**

\[SCREENSHOT: Email thread showing internal comments between team members alongside the customer conversation\]

Internal comments are Front's most differentiating collaboration feature. Within any email conversation, team members post comments that are completely invisible to the customer. The comments appear inline alongside the email thread—contextually relevant, immediately visible, and permanently attached to the conversation.

The practical impact transformed how our team coordinates. Before Front: "Hey Sarah, can you look at this email from Acme Corp? They're asking about enterprise pricing. I'm not sure if the Q3 discount still applies." This would require a separate Slack message, forwarded email, or walking to Sarah's desk. With Front: the agent posts an internal comment directly in the conversation, @mentions Sarah, and Sarah responds in the same thread with the answer—all without the customer seeing any of the discussion.

Our most common internal comment use cases: clarification requests ("Is this customer eligible for the loyalty discount?"), escalation context ("This customer has emailed 3 times about this issue—please prioritize"), manager approval ("Can you approve this $500 refund before I respond?"), and handoff instructions ("I'm out tomorrow—can someone follow up on this?"). Each comment creates a permanent record of the team's decision-making alongside the customer conversation.

The @mention system notifies specific team members when they're needed. Unlike email forwarding (which creates separate threads and loses context), @mentions keep the discussion in the original conversation where all context—customer messages, internal comments, and previous responses—is visible.

**Pro Tip:** Use internal comments for every decision that influences your response. When a future agent handles the same customer, the comment history explains not just what was said but why—the reasoning, the approvals, and the context that informed the response. This institutional knowledge is invaluable for teams with staff turnover or rotating assignments.

### **4.3 Rules & Automation - Operational Efficiency**

\[SCREENSHOT: Rules engine showing multi-condition routing and auto-assignment configuration\]

Front's rules engine automates the operational tasks that would otherwise consume team time: routing conversations to the right team member, tagging by category, setting priority, sending auto-responses, and escalating based on time conditions.

We configured 18 rules covering automatic assignment (conversations from enterprise domains → assign to account manager), tagging (subject containing "refund" or "cancel" → tag "retention"), SLA enforcement (conversation without response for 3 hours → move to urgent queue and notify team lead), out-of-hours response (conversations received outside business hours → send acknowledgment with expected response time), and follow-up reminders (conversation snoozed for 48 hours → resurface to assigned agent's inbox).

The rules builder supports multi-condition logic (if sender domain is X AND subject contains Y AND inbox is Z → then assign to agent and tag). The conditions cover conversation properties (sender, subject, body keywords, inbox, time received), and the actions include assignment, tagging, archiving, moving between inboxes, sending templates, and webhook triggers.

**What's Missing:** The rules engine is capable for email routing but doesn't match Zendesk's trigger/automation sophistication for complex multi-step workflows. Front's rules are single-action (one rule → one outcome). Zendesk's triggers can chain multiple actions and fire based on ticket lifecycle events that Front's email model doesn't track.

### **4.4 Analytics - Measuring Team Performance**

\[SCREENSHOT: Analytics dashboard showing response time, resolution time, conversation volume, and agent performance\]

Front's analytics (Growth and above) track the metrics that operations teams need: average response time (first response and subsequent), conversation volume (trends, busiest times, channel distribution), team performance (per-agent metrics for response time, volume handled, and CSAT), and SLA compliance (percentage of conversations meeting response time targets).

Our management dashboard showed 5 key metrics daily: average first-response time (target: under 2 hours), SLA compliance percentage (target: 95%), unassigned conversation count (target: zero at end of day), per-agent conversation volume (tracking workload distribution), and customer satisfaction trend (CSAT scores over time).

The tag-based reporting enabled analysis by category. We tagged conversations by topic (billing, technical, feature-request, onboarding), and the analytics showed volume and response time breakdowns per tag—identifying that billing conversations had the fastest response time (1.2 hours average) while technical conversations had the slowest (3.8 hours average). This insight led us to assign a dedicated technical specialist, reducing technical response time to 2.1 hours.

**What's Missing:** Custom reporting is available but not as flexible as Zendesk Explore. Cross-conversation analytics (how many conversations does the average customer create?) and cohort analysis (how does response time correlate with customer retention?) require data export to external BI tools.

### **4.5 Integrations - Connecting Email to Business Context**

\[SCREENSHOT: Integration sidebar showing Salesforce data, Asana tasks, and Slack notifications alongside a conversation\]

Front integrates with CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot—Growth and above), project management (Asana, Jira, Trello), communication (Slack, Microsoft Teams), and 100+ other tools through native integrations and Zapier. The integrations bring business context into conversations—agents see customer data, create tasks, and post notifications without leaving Front.

The Salesforce integration was the most impactful for our team. The conversation sidebar shows the customer's Salesforce record: account value, current opportunities, support history, and account manager. When a $50,000-value customer emails about a service issue, the agent immediately sees the revenue impact and handles the conversation with appropriate urgency—without opening Salesforce in a separate tab.

The Slack integration posts conversation events (new assignment, SLA breach, customer escalation) to Slack channels. Our #support-alerts channel receives notifications for unassigned conversations older than 30 minutes and SLA breaches—ensuring urgent items don't fall through cracks.

The Jira integration creates issues directly from Front conversations. When a customer reports a bug, the agent creates a Jira ticket from within the conversation, linking the customer's email to the development issue. The customer receives a follow-up when the Jira issue is resolved—closing the loop between customer communication and engineering execution.

### **4.6 Multi-Channel Expansion**

\[SCREENSHOT: Front handling email, SMS, and chat conversations in the unified inbox\]

Front has expanded beyond email into SMS, WhatsApp, live chat (via integrations), and social media. The multi-channel conversations appear in the same inbox alongside email, with the same assignment, commenting, and rules capabilities.

We tested SMS integration for transactional notifications and received modest volume (50-100 SMS conversations/month). The SMS experience within Front is functional—conversations thread naturally and agents respond from the same interface. The volume didn't justify the additional channel cost for our team, but organizations with SMS-heavy communication (logistics, appointment scheduling, field service) would find the unified inbox valuable.

**What's Missing:** The multi-channel capabilities are growing but email remains Front's strongest channel. Live chat is available through integrations (not native), social media support is basic compared to Zendesk's social channel depth, and phone support is not available. Teams needing chat-first or phone-first support should evaluate Intercom, Zendesk, or Freshdesk.

## **6. Front Cons: The Honest Limitations**

\[VISUAL: Cons summary infographic highlighting main pain points\]

### **Pricing Gets Expensive at Scale**

Growth at $59/seat includes every team member who responds—not just support agents. A 20-person team across support, sales, and operations costs $1,180/month. For teams where most members only occasionally need shared inbox access, the per-seat model creates cost friction.

### **No AI Chatbot for Automated Resolution**

Front doesn't include an AI chatbot that resolves customer inquiries automatically. Intercom's Fin (42% resolution) and Freshdesk's Freddy (18% resolution) deflect routine inquiries that Front routes entirely to human agents. For organizations wanting automated deflection, Front requires a separate AI tool.

### **Email-First Limits Multi-Channel Depth**

Chat, SMS, and social channels are available but secondary to the email-native experience. Teams whose primary support channel is live chat should use Intercom or Crisp. Teams needing phone support need a separate tool entirely.

### **Not a Traditional Help Desk**

No ticket lifecycle management (new → open → pending → solved), no formal SLA tiers with escalation trees, no advanced ticket routing based on issue type. Front's model is email conversations with team coordination—not structured ticket processing. Teams needing ticket management structure should use Zendesk or Freshdesk.

### **Learning Curve for the "In Between" Model**

Front isn't email and it isn't a help desk—it's something in between. Teams transitioning from Gmail need to learn Front's coordination features. Teams transitioning from Zendesk need to unlearn ticket-centric thinking. The adaptation period is 1-2 weeks for most teams.

### **Starter Plan Is Minimally Useful**

At $19/seat, Starter lacks SLA management, CRM integration, and custom reporting—features most teams need within the first month. The effective starting plan is Growth at $59/seat, making the $19 entry price somewhat misleading.

**Caution:** Budget for Growth ($59/seat) from the start if you need SLA tracking or CRM integration. The Starter-to-Growth upgrade is a 3x price increase for features that most operational teams consider essential.

**Where Zendesk Wins:** Structured ticket management with sophisticated SLA workflows, built-in phone support, AI chatbot (Answer Bot + Advanced AI), multi-channel depth (email, chat, phone, social all natively), larger marketplace (1,500+ apps), and enterprise compliance features.

**Where Front Wins:** Stronger team collaboration features (internal comments, shared drafts, @mentions are more developed), better multi-inbox management (sales + support + billing), CRM integration on Growth, and more sophisticated rules engine.

**Choose Help Scout if:** Your primary need is simple email support with a knowledge base and you value simplicity and lower pricing.

### **Front vs Gmail/Outlook Shared Mailbox: Platform vs DIY**

**Where Gmail/Outlook Wins:** Free (included with Google Workspace or Microsoft 365), familiar interface, no additional tool to learn, and no per-seat costs beyond what you already pay.

**Where Front Wins:** Everything collaborative—assignment, collision detection, internal comments, SLA tracking, analytics, CRM integration, and rules-based automation. Shared Gmail/Outlook collapses under 50+ daily conversations; Front handles thousands.

**Choose Gmail/Outlook if:** Your shared inbox handles fewer than 20 conversations per day and coordination problems haven't emerged yet.

**Choose Front if:** Your shared inbox exceeds 50 daily conversations, duplicate responses occur, or you need operational visibility into team performance.

### **What is Front?**

A collaborative inbox platform for managing shared email addresses (support@, sales@, etc.) with team coordination features: assignment, collision detection, internal comments, and shared drafts. The customer receives personal emails; the team works in a coordinated workspace.

Front manages email conversations with personal customer experience. Zendesk manages tickets with structured workflows. Choose Front for collaborative email; choose Zendesk for structured ticketing with multi-channel depth.

### **Is Front a help desk?**

Front sits between email and help desk. It provides some help desk features (assignment, SLA, analytics) without the ticket structure (no ticket numbers, no ticket statuses, no ticket routing trees). It's a collaborative inbox, not a traditional help desk.

### **Does Front have a chatbot?**

No native AI chatbot. Customer inquiries route to human agents. For automated AI resolution, use Intercom (Fin) or Freshdesk (Freddy) alongside or instead of Front.

### **Can Front handle phone calls?**

Not natively. Phone support requires a separate tool (Aircall, Dialpad, RingCentral). For built-in phone, use Zendesk or Freshdesk.

### **How many shared inboxes can Front manage?**

Starter: up to 10. Growth: 25. Scale: 50. Premier: unlimited. Each inbox can have different team members, rules, and SLA targets.

### **Does Front work with Salesforce?**

Yes—on Growth ($59/seat) and above. The integration shows Salesforce contact, account, and opportunity data in the conversation sidebar.

### **Is Front HIPAA compliant?**

On Scale ($99/seat) and above with BAA. Starter and Growth are not HIPAA eligible.

Help Scout is simpler and cheaper for email-first support with a built-in knowledge base. Front has stronger team collaboration (internal comments, shared drafts) and better multi-inbox management. Yes—sales teams commonly use Front for managing sales@ inboxes, coordinating prospect responses, and tracking response times to inbound leads. The CRM integration provides deal context.

### **Does Front have a knowledge base?**

No native knowledge base. For self-service documentation, use a separate tool (Help Scout Docs, Zendesk Guide, or Notion) and link from Front conversations.
