## **1. Introduction**

GetResponse's unique proposition is combining email marketing with webinars, conversion funnels, and landing pages in one platform—a combination no other major email tool offers. After four months running it for an online education business, I found the all-in-one approach genuinely reduces tool sprawl for businesses that rely on webinar-to-sale workflows. Our webinar → follow-up email → sales page → checkout funnel ran entirely within GetResponse—no Zoom, no separate landing page tool, no funnel builder.

Founded in 1998 by Simon Grabowski in Poland, GetResponse is one of the oldest email marketing platforms, serving over 350,000 customers globally. The platform has evolved from a simple email tool into a broader marketing platform, with webinars being its most distinctive feature.

The all-in-one approach works well for businesses whose marketing centers on webinars and email funnels. For businesses needing deep email automation (ActiveCampaign is better), e-commerce email (Klaviyo is better), or the simplest email experience (Mailchimp is better), GetResponse's jack-of-all-trades positioning means it's good at everything but best at nothing—except webinars.

## **2. Key Features & Verdict**

**Webinars:** Built-in webinar hosting with registration, email reminders, live streaming, screen sharing, polls, and recording. No Zoom subscription needed. Our webinars hosted 200+ attendees reliably. The webinar-to-email-sequence workflow was seamless.

**Conversion Funnels:** Pre-built funnel templates (lead magnet, sales, webinar, product launch) with landing pages, email sequences, and payment processing. The visual funnel builder shows the complete journey from ad/opt-in through email nurture to sale.

**Email Marketing:** Standard campaign builder with 120+ templates, A/B testing, and send-time optimization. The email capabilities are comparable to Mailchimp—solid without being exceptional.

**Marketing Automation:** Visual workflow builder on the Marketing Automation plan. Handles standard automations (welcome series, lead scoring, behavioral triggers). Less sophisticated than ActiveCampaign but adequate for standard workflows.

**Landing Pages:** Drag-and-drop builder with templates optimized for conversion. The pages work well for webinar registration, lead magnets, and simple sales pages. Not as flexible as Unbounce or Webflow.

### **Overall Rating: 3.7/5**

GetResponse is the best choice for businesses whose marketing revolves around webinars and email funnels. The built-in webinar capability is unique among email platforms. The conversion funnel builder reduces tool sprawl for businesses running webinar-to-sale workflows.

For businesses not using webinars, the platform's value proposition weakens. ActiveCampaign has better automation. Mailchimp has better templates. Klaviyo has better e-commerce. GetResponse's strength is specifically the webinar + email + funnel combination.

**Best For:** Online educators, coaches, and course creators running webinar-based marketing.

**Not Recommended For:** E-commerce (use Klaviyo), pure email marketing (use Mailchimp), or automation-focused teams (use ActiveCampaign).
