## **2. What is Klaviyo? Understanding the Platform**

\[VISUAL: Klaviyo's e-commerce data model showing product, customer, and behavioral data flowing into email/SMS\]

Klaviyo is an email and SMS marketing platform built specifically for e-commerce. The platform ingests data from your online store — products, orders, customer behavior, website visits, cart activity — enriches it with predictive analytics (lifetime value, churn risk, next purchase prediction), and uses it to power personalized email and SMS campaigns that drive revenue.

What fundamentally separates Klaviyo from Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or Brevo is the depth of e-commerce data integration. Klaviyo doesn't just know that a customer bought something — it knows what they bought, when, how much they've spent lifetime, what products they've browsed since their last purchase, whether they're likely to purchase again based on their individual pattern, and approximately when that next purchase will happen. This data powers segmentation capabilities that simply aren't possible on general email platforms — segments like "customers who bought Product A but haven't purchased in 60 days, with a lifetime value above $200, who have browsed our new collection in the last week."

The platform combines email marketing, SMS marketing, push notifications, and reviews/forms into a unified system. Flows (automated sequences triggered by behavior) and campaigns (one-time sends to segments) operate on the same customer data with the same segmentation engine, creating a consistent marketing experience across channels.

Klaviyo's data model treats every interaction as a marketing signal. A product page view updates the customer's interest profile. An abandoned cart triggers a recovery sequence. A purchase updates lifetime value, refreshes product recommendations, and adjusts the customer's segment membership — all automatically, all in real time. This event-driven architecture means your marketing becomes more personalized with every customer interaction, without manual intervention.

## **3. Klaviyo Pricing & Plans: Contact-Based Scaling**

\[VISUAL: Pricing calculator showing cost by contact count\]

Klaviyo's pricing is contact-based and scales with your active profile count. Understanding the pricing model is important because the costs can surprise you as your email list grows, but so can the revenue.

### **3.1 Free Plan (250 Contacts, 500 Emails/Month) — Genuine Evaluation**

The free plan includes the full flow builder, campaigns, segmentation, and analytics — limited only by the 250-contact cap. For new stores just starting their email program, this is enough to build and test your initial flows (welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase) before committing budget. We built our entire flow architecture on the free plan before upgrading, which let us test the platform's capabilities with real customer data before paying.

### **3.2 Email Plans ($20/month Starting) — Growing Stores**

Email plans scale by contact count: $20/month for 251-500 contacts, with pricing increasing as your list grows. All email features are included at every tier — flows, campaigns, A/B testing, segmentation, predictive analytics, and reporting. The per-contact pricing model means you pay for the size of your engaged audience, not for feature access.

### **3.3 Email + SMS Plans ($35/month Starting) — Multi-Channel**

SMS adds text messaging and MMS as channels alongside email, sharing the same customer data and segmentation engine. SMS credits are purchased separately ($0.01-0.02 per SMS in the US, higher internationally). The combined plan ensures email and SMS are coordinated — you can set rules preventing a customer from receiving both an email and an SMS about the same promotion within the same time window.

## **4. Key Features Deep Dive**

### **4.1 Flows — Behavioral Email Automation for E-Commerce**

\[SCREENSHOT: Flow builder showing an abandoned cart sequence with conditional splits based on cart value\]

Klaviyo's flows are automated email/SMS sequences triggered by customer behavior, and they're the platform's highest-revenue feature. The flow builder provides conditional splits, time delays, A/B testing within flows, dynamic content blocks, and channel mixing (email + SMS in the same flow) — all designed for e-commerce use cases.

Our highest-revenue flow was the abandoned cart sequence, and the sophistication it allowed went far beyond what we'd achieved on Mailchimp. When a customer added products to cart but didn't check out within one hour, Klaviyo sent a personalized reminder showing the exact products in their cart — with images, prices, and a direct link to checkout. If they didn't convert within 24 hours, a second email included a 10% discount, but only for first-time buyers (conditional split based on purchase count = 0). For returning customers, the second email emphasized free shipping instead. If they still didn't convert after 48 hours, a final email created urgency with limited stock messaging — but only if the product actually had inventory below a threshold (conditional split based on real-time inventory data from Shopify). This three-email flow recovered $4,200/month in otherwise-lost revenue.

Other high-performing flows included: a 5-email welcome series (brand story → product education → social proof → first-purchase incentive → preference collection) that converted 12% of new subscribers to buyers; post-purchase flows timed to estimated delivery dates (thank you → shipping tracking → usage tips → review request → cross-sell recommendations); browse abandonment targeting visitors who viewed products but didn't add to cart ($1,800/month in generated revenue); and win-back flows re-engaging customers who hadn't purchased in 90+ days with personalized product recommendations based on their individual purchase history.

The pre-built flow templates accelerated our setup significantly. Klaviyo provides templates for every common e-commerce automation — we started with templates, customized the copy and design for our brand, adjusted the conditional logic for our specific customer segments, and had 8 flows running within the first week.

### **4.2 Segmentation — E-Commerce-Grade Targeting**

\[SCREENSHOT: Segment builder showing purchase behavior, browsing, and predictive data conditions\]

Klaviyo's segmentation is where the e-commerce data depth becomes tangible, and it's the feature that most justifies the premium pricing. Segments can combine purchase history (bought specific product, total orders, average order value, last purchase date, total lifetime revenue), browsing behavior (viewed specific products, browsed specific categories, visit frequency), email engagement (opened, clicked, converted from email), SMS engagement (clicked, replied), predictive data (predicted next order date, churn risk score, predicted lifetime value), and any custom properties you've added.

Our most effective segment: "Customers who purchased from Collection X in the last 90 days AND have a predicted lifetime value above $300 AND haven't purchased in 45+ days AND have opened at least 1 email in the last 30 days." This segment received a personalized "new arrivals in Collection X" email with dynamic product recommendations pulled from the same collection. The campaign to this 800-person segment achieved a 6.8% conversion rate versus 1.2% for our general list — 5.6x higher conversion driven entirely by behavioral segmentation.

Segments update in real time. When a customer makes a purchase, their segment membership refreshes immediately — they exit the "hasn't purchased in 45 days" segment and enter the "recent buyer" segment, which changes which flows and campaigns they receive. This dynamic membership prevents embarrassing situations like sending a discount to someone who just paid full price five minutes ago.

### **4.3 Predictive Analytics — AI for E-Commerce Decision-Making**

\[SCREENSHOT: Customer profile showing predicted lifetime value, churn risk, and next order prediction\]

Klaviyo's predictive analytics calculate three metrics per customer: expected date of next order, predicted customer lifetime value, and churn risk probability. These predictions are based on each customer's individual purchase history compared to behavioral patterns across your entire customer base, and they become more accurate as Klaviyo collects more data about your store's specific customer behavior.

We used churn risk scoring to trigger win-back automations proactively — reaching customers showing churn signals before they fully lapsed. When a customer's churn risk exceeded 70%, they entered a win-back flow with personalized product recommendations and a targeted incentive. The proactive approach retained an estimated 8% of at-risk customers who would have churned without intervention, representing approximately $15,000 in preserved lifetime value over six months.

Predicted lifetime value powered our VIP segmentation. Customers with predicted CLV above $500 received different treatment — early access to new products, exclusive discount codes, and personalized thank-you messages. This tiered approach drove our highest-value customers to spend 35% more than they would have without the VIP treatment.

### **4.4 SMS Marketing — The Higher-Converting Channel**

Klaviyo's SMS adds text messaging as a parallel channel alongside email, sharing the same customer data, segmentation engine, and automation flows. SMS campaigns support conversation-style messaging (two-way texting), MMS with images and GIFs, and full compliance tools including opt-in management, quiet hours enforcement, and automatic TCPA compliance.

Our SMS campaigns achieved 25% click-through rates — dramatically higher than email's 3-4% — making SMS the higher-converting channel for time-sensitive promotions like flash sales, back-in-stock alerts, and limited-time offers. The per-message cost ($0.01-0.02 per SMS in the US) means SMS is best reserved for high-intent, high-value messages rather than routine newsletters. We sent SMS to our most engaged segment (~8,000 contacts) for flash sales and achieved 4x the revenue per send compared to email-only campaigns.

### **4.5 Revenue Attribution — Proving Email's Value**

\[SCREENSHOT: Revenue dashboard showing attributed revenue by flow and campaign\]

Klaviyo tracks revenue attributed to every email and SMS using a configurable attribution window (default: 5-day click, 1-day open). Our dashboard showed exactly which flows, campaigns, and individual messages generated purchases — down to the specific subject line variant in an A/B test. The $180,000+ in attributable revenue over six months gave our marketing team concrete ROI data for budget discussions, and the granularity allowed us to optimize underperforming flows based on revenue contribution rather than vanity metrics like open rates.

The attribution data transformed our email strategy. We discovered that our browse abandonment flow (which we'd almost disabled due to low open rates) was actually our third-highest revenue generator, because the customers who did open had very high purchase intent. Without revenue attribution, we would have killed a $1,800/month revenue stream based on misleading engagement metrics.

## **6. Klaviyo Cons: Where It Falls Short**

### **E-Commerce Only — Narrow by Design**

Klaviyo is built for online stores, and its value evaporates outside that context. B2B companies, SaaS businesses, content creators, and non-e-commerce organizations get no advantage over general email platforms — and may actually find the e-commerce-centric interface confusing. The focus is a strength for stores and a hard limitation for everyone else.

### **Pricing Is Premium and Scales Aggressively**

At 35,000 contacts, Klaviyo costs ~$460/month. Mailchimp Standard costs ~$270. ActiveCampaign Plus costs ~$209. Brevo costs ~$65. The pricing premium requires revenue attribution to justify, and for small stores with modest email revenue, the math may not work. I've seen stores switch to Klaviyo expecting magic and find that their email program wasn't mature enough to leverage the advanced features — they paid 3x more for the same basic campaigns they were already sending.

### **Complexity Overwhelms Simple Needs**

If you just want to send a monthly newsletter to your customer list, Klaviyo's data depth, flow builder, and segmentation engine are overkill. The interface assumes you want sophisticated behavioral automation, and simpler tasks sometimes require more clicks than they should. Mailchimp or Brevo handle straightforward campaigns with less cognitive overhead.

### **Advanced Flow Building Has a Learning Curve**

Building sophisticated conditional flows with A/B tests, dynamic content, and multi-channel logic takes genuine time to learn. The pre-built templates help for standard use cases, but customizing flows for your specific customer segments and business logic requires understanding Klaviyo's data model, event system, and conditional syntax. Budget 2-3 weeks for your team to become proficient with advanced flows.

### **SMS Costs Can Surprise You**

SMS is billed per message ($0.01-0.02 per SMS in the US, $0.05-0.10 internationally). A campaign to 10,000 contacts costs $100-200 on top of your subscription. MMS messages (with images) cost more. High-volume SMS programs can double your effective Klaviyo spend, and the per-message pricing isn't always obvious during campaign setup.

## **15. Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Is Klaviyo free?**
Yes, for up to 250 contacts with 500 emails/month. The free plan includes flows, campaigns, and segmentation — it's a full-featured evaluation environment, not a stripped-down trial. Paid plans start at $20/month for 251-500 contacts.

### **How does Klaviyo compare to Mailchimp for e-commerce?**
Klaviyo has dramatically deeper e-commerce data integration, more sophisticated segmentation, predictive analytics, and precise revenue attribution. Mailchimp is easier to learn, cheaper at every tier, and has a more generous free plan. Choose Mailchimp for standard e-commerce emails; choose Klaviyo when email is a primary revenue channel and you want data-driven personalization.

### **Does Klaviyo work with Shopify?**
Yes — Klaviyo is Shopify's recommended email platform with the deepest native integration available. One-click installation from the Shopify app store with real-time data sync for products, customers, orders, and website behavior. The integration is the most seamless we've tested across any email platform.

### **Is Klaviyo only for e-commerce?**
Primarily yes. The platform's value comes entirely from e-commerce data integration — purchase history, product catalogs, cart behavior, and predictive analytics built on transactional data. Non-e-commerce businesses should use ActiveCampaign, HubSpot, or Mailchimp.

### **Does Klaviyo include SMS marketing?**
Yes — Email+SMS plans combine both channels with shared customer data, segmentation, and automation. SMS is billed per message ($0.01-0.02 per SMS in the US) on top of the base subscription. SMS and email can be coordinated within the same flows with frequency capping across channels.

### **What are Klaviyo flows?**
Automated email/SMS sequences triggered by customer behavior — abandoned cart, welcome series, post-purchase, browse abandonment, win-back, and custom triggers. Flows run continuously and automatically, and they're typically the highest-revenue feature of the platform. Our flows generated approximately $12,500/month in attributable revenue.

### **How does Klaviyo's pricing work?**
Contact-based pricing on "active profiles" — contacts you've emailed or texted in the billing period. Inactive contacts don't count toward your plan. Pricing scales with your list size, and all features are available at every paid tier.

### **Can I migrate from Mailchimp to Klaviyo?**
Yes. Klaviyo provides a dedicated Mailchimp migration tool that imports contacts, lists, segments, and templates. Historical engagement data transfers, preserving your sender reputation. Plan for 1-2 weeks for migration and rebuilding flows in Klaviyo's builder.

### **How accurate is Klaviyo's revenue attribution?**
Attribution uses configurable windows — default is 5-day click and 1-day open attribution. This means a purchase within 5 days of clicking an email link or 1 day of opening an email is attributed to that email. The methodology is standard for email marketing platforms, though multi-touch attribution (when a customer interacts with multiple emails before purchasing) credits the most recent touchpoint.

### **What e-commerce platforms does Klaviyo support?**
Shopify (deepest integration), WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, and others. Shopify receives the most development attention and has the most seamless integration. Non-Shopify platforms work well but may require more manual configuration.

### **Is Klaviyo worth the premium pricing?**
For e-commerce brands where email drives significant revenue (20%+ of total), yes — our 65x ROI speaks for itself. For small stores, new stores, or stores where email is a minor channel, the premium is harder to justify. Start with Mailchimp or Omnisend and migrate to Klaviyo when your email program matures enough to leverage the advanced features.

### **Does Klaviyo support product reviews?**
Yes. Klaviyo Reviews is a built-in feature that collects and displays product reviews, integrating review data into customer profiles for segmentation. You can trigger review requests through flows timed to estimated delivery dates and use review data (positive reviewers, negative reviewers) as segmentation criteria.
