## **1. Introduction**

Noloco builds client-facing portals from Airtable and Google Sheets with built-in user authentication, automation, and a focus on professional service businesses. The platform differentiates from Softr through stronger automation capabilities and from Pory through user authentication and interactive features.

After three months building client portals for a consulting firm, I found Noloco excels at a specific workflow: taking project data from Airtable, exposing it to clients through branded portals, and automating notifications and updates. The client experience is polished—each client logs in and sees only their projects, files, and communication history. The automation triggers emails when project milestones are reached.

Noloco sits between Softr (more polished design) and Stacker (more data source flexibility) in the Airtable portal builder space. Noloco's automation capabilities—triggered by data changes, form submissions, or scheduled events—are the feature that distinguishes it for service businesses with active client communication needs.

## **2. Verdict**

### **Overall Rating: 3.5/5**

Noloco delivers well for agencies and service businesses needing automated client portals from Airtable. The automation is the differentiator against Softr and Pory. The limitation is Airtable dependency and narrower applicability compared to more general platforms.

**Best For:** Agencies building automated client portals from Airtable.

**Not Recommended For:** General application building or non-Airtable data sources.
