---

## **1. Introduction**

Pory builds simple websites and portals from Airtable data. It's simpler and more limited than Softr (its closest competitor) but serves the basic use case of turning an Airtable base into a public or members-only website with search, filtering, and responsive design.

After two months building two portals, I found Pory delivers on its simple premise adequately. Our member directory went from Airtable base to live portal in about 2 hours. The resource portal with categorization and search took half a day. Both looked professional and functioned reliably.

The platform's scope is narrow: display Airtable data through templates with search and filtering. No user authentication beyond basic password protection, no forms for data submission, and no application logic. For teams needing more than data display, Softr provides significantly more capability at a comparable price.

## **2. Verdict**

### **Overall Rating: 3.3/5**

Pory is the simplest way to create a public portal from Airtable data. The limitation is that simplicity—it's a data display layer without authentication, forms, or logic that Softr provides. For basic directories and resource portals, it works. For anything more complex, use Softr.

**Best For:** Simple Airtable-powered directories and portals.

**Not Recommended For:** Applications needing user auth, forms, or business logic.
