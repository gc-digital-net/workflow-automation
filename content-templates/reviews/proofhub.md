## **2. What is ProofHub?**

ProofHub is an all-in-one project management and team collaboration platform with flat-rate pricing. The platform combines task management (boards, lists, Gantt), team discussions, file sharing, proofing and approval tools, time tracking, and custom reports in a single interface.

The platform targets agencies and SMBs—teams large enough that per-user pricing feels punitive but not large enough for enterprise platforms. The "all-in-one" approach means you don't need separate tools for task management (Asana), proofing (InVision), time tracking (Harvest), and discussions (Slack)—ProofHub handles all of these, albeit at a lower depth than dedicated tools.

The proofing feature is ProofHub's second differentiator after pricing. Upload images, PDFs, or documents, and reviewers can add markup directly on the files with annotation tools. While not as sophisticated as Wrike's proofing, it serves the basic creative review workflow that agencies need.

## **4. Key Features**

### **4.1 Task Management**

ProofHub offers multiple task views: board (Kanban), table, Gantt chart, and calendar. Tasks support assignees, due dates, priorities, labels, subtasks, and time estimates. The task management is functional without being exceptional—comparable to Basecamp's simplicity rather than ClickUp's depth.

We managed 20+ client projects with task lists organized by project phase. The Kanban boards served daily workflow management. The Gantt charts helped with project scheduling. The experience was adequate for our agency's needs without the sophistication that complex projects would demand.

### **4.2 Built-In Proofing**

Upload creative assets and reviewers markup directly on the files. The proofing tools include annotation, drawing, text comments positioned on specific image coordinates, and version comparison. The feature isn't as deep as Wrike's (no video proofing, less sophisticated markup tools) but serves the basic creative review workflow without a separate tool like InVision.

### **4.3 Time Tracking**

Built-in time tracking with manual entry and timer. Time logs associate with tasks and projects. Basic timesheet reports show hours by project, team member, and date. The time tracking is functional—not as capable as Harvest or Toggl, but adequate for teams needing basic hour tracking without a separate subscription.

### **4.4 Discussions & Chat**

Built-in discussions (threaded conversations by topic) and real-time chat replace the need for separate communication tools for project-related conversations. The discussions are project-context—they live within the project, not in a separate app.

### **4.5 Custom Roles & Permissions**

Create custom roles with granular permissions controlling who can view, edit, and manage different project elements. The permission system is more flexible than most flat-rate tools and serves agencies managing client access alongside team access.

## **6. ProofHub Cons**

### **Interface Feels Dated** - ProofHub's design trails the modern aesthetic of Asana, Monday.com, or Linear. The interface is functional but not inspiring. Team members accustomed to modern tools may resist adoption.

### **Feature Depth Is Shallow** - Each feature (tasks, proofing, time tracking) works but doesn't match dedicated tools. Wrike's proofing is deeper. Harvest's time tracking is more capable. Asana's task management is more sophisticated.

### **Limited Integration Ecosystem** - Fewer integrations than any major competitor. Basic connections to Slack, Google Drive, and Dropbox exist, but the app marketplace is minimal. Zapier bridges gaps.

### **No Free Plan** - $45/month minimum. For small teams (under 10), per-user tools like ClickUp ($7/user = $70 for 10) are cheaper. The flat pricing advantage only kicks in above 10-15 users.

### **Mobile App Is Basic** - The mobile experience handles viewing and basic updates but isn't designed for productive mobile work. Trello and Pipedrive have better mobile apps.

### **Reporting Is Limited** - Basic task and time reports. No custom dashboards, no cross-project analytics, no advanced data visualization. Management reporting needs may outgrow ProofHub quickly.
