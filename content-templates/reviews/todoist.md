## The Todoist Philosophy: Clarity Through Simplicity

Todoist's design principles shape every feature:

**Capture Quickly:**
The faster you can get tasks out of your head, the more useful a task manager becomes. Todoist optimizes for instant capture with natural language input, keyboard shortcuts, and ubiquitous quick-add across platforms.

**Organize Intuitively:**
Projects and labels provide hierarchy without complexity. The system is obvious enough that you don't need training, flexible enough to adapt to any methodology (GTD, time blocking, simple lists).

**Complete Confidently:**
Clear priorities, smart scheduling, and productivity tracking (Karma) help you focus on what matters and feel accomplished when you finish.

**Sync Everywhere:**
Tasks captured on your phone appear on your computer instantly. The same experience across web, desktop, mobile, and even wearables.

**Independence Matters:**
As a bootstrapped company, Todoist can prioritize long-term user value over short-term growth metrics. Features are built for utility, not engagement metrics.

## Task Management: The Core Experience

Tasks are Todoist's fundamental unit:

**Creating Tasks:**
Natural language input makes task creation fast:
- "Call dentist tomorrow at 2pm" → Task with date and time
- "Review proposal every Monday" → Recurring task
- "Buy groceries p1 #shopping @errands" → Priority, project, label
- "Submit report in 3 days" → Relative date

The parser handles:
- Dates (tomorrow, Monday, Jan 15, next week)
- Times (at 2pm, 3:00, noon)
- Recurring (every day, every other Monday, every 3rd Friday)
- Priorities (p1, p2, p3, p4)
- Projects (#project_name)
- Labels (@label_name)
- Assignees (+person_name)

**Task Properties:**
- **Task name:** What needs to be done
- **Description:** Additional details (markdown supported)
- **Due date:** When it's due
- **Priority:** P1 (urgent) to P4 (no priority)
- **Project:** Where it belongs
- **Labels:** Cross-project categorization
- **Reminders:** Notifications before due
- **Assignee:** Team member responsible
- **Comments:** Discussion thread
- **Sub-tasks:** Nested task breakdown
- **Attachments:** Files and images

**Due Dates:**
Todoist's date handling is excellent:
- Specific dates (Jan 15)
- Relative dates (tomorrow, next Monday)
- Recurring (every week, every 2 days, every workday)
- Complex recurring (every last Friday)
- No date (someday/maybe items)

**Recurring Tasks:**
One of Todoist's strengths:
- Daily/weekly/monthly/yearly
- Every X days/weeks/months
- Specific days (every Mon, Wed, Fri)
- Complex patterns (every 2nd Tuesday)
- "Every!" for fixed dates vs "Every" for completion-based

**Priorities:**
Four priority levels with visual indicators:
- P1 (Red): Urgent/important
- P2 (Orange): High priority
- P3 (Blue): Medium priority
- P4 (None): No specific priority

Priorities affect sort order in views.

**Sub-tasks:**
Break complex tasks into steps:
- Indent under parent task
- Track completion separately
- Parent shows progress
- Unlimited nesting depth

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Todoist free plan usable long-term?**
Yes, for simple needs. You get 5 projects, basic features, and core functionality. Most people upgrade for reminders, unlimited projects, and labels. But casual users can operate on free indefinitely.

**How does Todoist compare to Apple Reminders?**
Todoist is significantly more powerful: natural language, cross-platform, projects, labels, filters, recurring tasks, and collaboration. Apple Reminders is simpler and free but limited. Todoist is worth paying for if task management matters to you.

**Can Todoist replace a project management tool?**
For simple projects, yes. For complex project management with dependencies, resource allocation, and Gantt charts, no. Todoist is task management, not project management. Use both if needed.

**Does Todoist work offline?**
Yes, fully. You can view, create, edit, and complete tasks offline. Changes sync when you reconnect. This works on all platforms including mobile.

**Is Todoist good for teams?**
For small teams with simple collaboration needs, yes. For larger teams or those needing robust project management, consider Asana, Monday.com, or ClickUp. Todoist Business is best for teams of 10-15 or fewer.

**How do I use Todoist for GTD?**
Projects = Projects. Labels = Contexts (@home, @computer). Filters = Custom views. Inbox = Capture and process. Due dates for deadlines, priority for importance. Many GTD practitioners love Todoist.

**Can I integrate Todoist with my calendar?**
Yes. Google Calendar and Outlook support two-way sync on Pro/Business. Tasks appear as events, and changes sync both ways. Apple Calendar works via one-way iCal feed.

**What's the difference between Pro and Business?**
Pro is for individuals. Business adds team features: shared workspace, admin controls, team billing, role management, and priority support. Choose Business when multiple people need collaboration.

**Does Todoist have reminders?**
Yes, on Pro and Business. Set reminders relative to due date/time or at specific times. Location-based reminders on mobile too. Free plan doesn't include reminders.

**How do recurring tasks work?**
Powerful. "Every Monday," "every 2 weeks," "every 3rd Friday," "every weekday"—Todoist handles it. "Every" reschedules from completion date. "Every!" reschedules from original schedule. Covers almost any pattern.

**Can I use Todoist for habit tracking?**
Somewhat, using recurring tasks. But dedicated habit apps (Streaks, Habitica) are better for this. Todoist is optimized for tasks, not habit chains.

**Is my data safe with Todoist?**
Yes. Encrypted, regularly backed up, GDPR compliant. You can export all data anytime. The company is independently profitable and built for longevity—your data isn't going anywhere.
