## **1. Introduction: Open-Source Internal Tools, Beautifully Designed**

Tooljet distinguishes itself in the open-source internal tool space through design quality. While Appsmith and Budibase are functional, Tooljet's visual builder and generated application UIs are noticeably more polished—closer to Retool's commercial quality. After three months building 6 internal tools, I found Tooljet provides a pleasant building experience that makes it enjoyable to create internal tools rather than just productive.

The platform connects to 50+ data sources (databases, APIs, cloud services), provides 45+ UI components, supports JavaScript and Python for custom logic, and includes a visual workflow builder. The feature set competes with Appsmith and Budibase while providing a more refined visual experience.

Tooljet was founded in 2021 by Navaneeth PK and is licensed under AGPL v3. The company has raised $7 million and has grown rapidly through the open-source community. The platform is newer than Appsmith (2019) and Budibase (2020), which means fewer battle-tested deployments but also a more modern architecture.

## **2-4. Platform, Pricing & Features**

Tooljet is an open-source low-code platform for building internal tools and admin panels. It connects to PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB, REST APIs, GraphQL, Google Sheets, Airtable, Firebase, Stripe, and 40+ other data sources.

**Free (Self-Hosted):** Unlimited users, unlimited apps. AGPL v3 license.

**Cloud Free:** 5 users, unlimited apps.

**Business ($20/user/month):** SSO, audit logs, granular permissions.

**Enterprise (Custom):** SLA, priority support, advanced governance.

**Key features:** Visual app builder with 45+ components, JavaScript and Python support, visual workflow builder, database migrations, version control, and multi-page applications.

The visual builder is where Tooljet shines. Drag components onto a canvas, configure properties through a clean sidebar panel, and bind data with a few clicks. The layout system is more intuitive than Appsmith's, and the component designs are more modern than Budibase's. Tables, forms, charts, modals, tabs, and menus all render cleanly.

The workflow builder handles event-driven automations: database triggers, scheduled tasks, and webhook-triggered processes. The visual flow builder is cleaner than Budibase's automation interface, though less powerful than Retool's workflow engine.

## **5-6. Pros & Cons**

### **Pros**

### **Best-Looking Open-Source Internal Tools** - Generated applications look more polished than Appsmith or Budibase output. The visual quality is closer to Retool's commercial standard.

### **Modern Architecture** - Built from scratch in 2021 with modern web technologies. The codebase is cleaner and more maintainable than older platforms.

### **Affordable Business Tier** - $20/user/month for SSO and audit logs. Cheaper than Appsmith's $40/user business tier.

### **JavaScript and Python Support** - Write custom logic in either language—more flexible than Appsmith (JavaScript only).

### **Visual Workflow Builder** - Built-in automation without a separate tool.

### **Cons**

### **Youngest Platform** - Founded 2021. Less battle-tested, fewer production deployments, and a smaller community than Appsmith.

### **AGPL v3 License** - More restrictive than Appsmith's Apache 2.0. Some enterprises have legal concerns about AGPL.

### **Smaller Component Library** - 45+ components vs Appsmith's 50+ and Retool's 100+. Growing but gaps exist.

### **Documentation Is Growing** - Improving rapidly but not yet comprehensive. Community resources are thinner than Appsmith's.

### **Fewer Data Source Connectors** - 50+ vs Appsmith's 25+ named connectors. The count is competitive but some niche databases may not be supported.
